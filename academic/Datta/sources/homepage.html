Anupam Datta

Assistant Research Professor, CyLab, ECE, and (by courtesy) CSD
Carnegie Mellon University

PhD 2005, MS 2002 (Computer Science, Stanford University) 
BTech 2000 (Computer Science & Engineering, IIT Kharagpur)

My research focuses on foundations of security and privacy. I belong to the Security group and am also affiliated with the Principles of Programming group. I participate in the TRUST and SHARPS centers and several Science of Security initiatives.

Program Co-Chair: 2013 IEEE Computer Security Foundations Symposium

Publications [DBLP] & Talks

Research Thrusts

  • Privacy, Audit and Accountability (Formalizing and Enforcing Privacy) [Overview Paper and Video Lecture]
    • Audit Games
    • Formalizing and Enforcing Purpose Restrictions (Application: Healthcare privacy)
    • Policy Auditing over Incomplete Logs (Application: Healthcare privacy, in particular, the HIPAA Privacy Rule)
    • Privacy and Contextual Integrity
  • Compositional Security (Modularity in Security)
    • Naturally Rehearsing Passwords (Composing Programs and People)
    • Compositional System Security with Interface-Confined Adversaries (Applications: Trusted computing systems, extensible hypervisors, network protocols)
    • Protocol Composition Logic(PCL) (Applications: Industrial authentication and key exchange protocols)
    • Program Equivalence, Games and Universal Composability
